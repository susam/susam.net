There are a few things to note here:

  • If it were only a single email notification like this, I could entertain the thought that this could be a genuine customer who mistyped the email address and entered mine by mistake. But 3 notifications, one activated mobile number, and one more in progress, I am now worried that this is an active attack going on!
  • There was no email verification whatsoever to confirm whether the person making the purchase (who I think is an attacker) really owns the email address they entered while making the purchase. Apparently, Reliance Jio allows anyone to purchase a new mobile number with anyone's email address without verifying whether the customer really owns the given email address by sending a link or OTP to the email address. This appears to be a bug in their registration workflow. I have reported this bug to them via their bug reporting email address (jio.bugsreporting at jio.com).
  • One might wonder if these are merely spam or phishing emails. I have confirmed that they are not. I have contacted the Reliance Customer Support team about it and while they acknowledge the problem, they are not making any progress in providing a resolution. They just keep asking me to call 1800-88-99999 but that is of no help to me because the first thing you need to do after calling that number is punch in your Jio number. Well, I don't have any Jio number and that is the whole issue here, is it not? I don't have any Jio mobile number. Someone else is buying Jio mobile numbers with my contact details. The customer support workflow appears to be totally dysfunctional for an issue of this nature.
